🥩 Robust Campground Dinner Playbook
🫘 Meal 8: One-Pot Campground Chili Mac
• Steps: Boil a generous portion of penne pasta noodles in your large pot until tender. Drain out
the liquid fully. Open your prepared can of bean chili and dump it directly over the hot noodles.
Stir over a low stove flame for 3 minutes until bubbling hot. Cut the flame, toss in shredded
cheddar cheese, and stir until completely gooey. Eat right out of the pot!
🥩 Meal 9: No-Pan Wilderness "Hobo" Beef Foil Packets
• Steps: Double-wrap two sheets of heavy foil. Layer a pile of diced potatoes, baby carrots, and
chopped onions in the center. Top with your home-frozen ground beef (now thawed). Season
with salt, pepper, and BBQ sauce. Seal tightly into an airtight pouch. Drop directly into the hot
glowing campfire embers using your tongs. Bake for 20-25 minutes, flipping once.
🥔 Meal 10: Searing Kielbasa & Potato Skillet Hash
• Steps: Slice your smoked kielbasa loop into thick coin discs. Drop into a dry skillet over
medium-high heat for 4 minutes until browned. Remove sausage. Drop two tablespoons of
butter and a fully drained can of sliced potatoes into the same sausage oil. Fry for 5 minutes
until crispy, toss the kielbasa back in with chopped onions, heat through, and serve!
🍍 Meal 11: BBQ Chicken & Pineapple Foil Packs
• Steps: Tear heavy foil sheets. Combine a drained can of chicken breast chunks, drained
canned pineapple chunks, diced bell peppers, and yellow onions in the center. Smother
generously with sweet BBQ sauce. Fold tightly into an airtight wrap and set onto the campfire
metal grate over hot coals for 15-20 minutes until the pineapple edges caramelize.
🪵 Meal 12: Fire pit Kielbasa & Cabbage Skillet Hash
• Steps: Toss kielbasa slices into a skillet over medium heat until they brown and release their
smoky savory oils. Dump in a heavy handful of yellow onions, bell peppers, and your
pre-shredded slaw/cabbage mix. Sauté frequently for 8 minutes until the cabbage wilts down
completely and absorbs the rich sausage drippings.
🍝 Meal 13: One-Pot Creamy Tomato Pesto Pasta
• Steps: Boil penne pasta until al dente and drain. Pour a jar of marinara sauce and 3
tablespoons of basil pesto directly over the pasta. Stir over a low burner. Drop in a half block of
cream cheese or cream, stirring continuously until it melts into a rich, velvety pink pesto cream
sauce.
