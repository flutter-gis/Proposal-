● The Glacial Sand Filter: Note that the soil beneath the cabin loop is an ancient glacial

🪨 STATION 2 DOSSIER: PAWTUCKAWAY STATE PARK (CABIN 4)
🌋 Volcanic Caldera Ring Dike: Pawtuckaway is a world-class geological anomaly. The
horseshoe shape of North, Middle, and South Mountains forms a perfect, circular ring because
you are physically standing inside the collapsed structural ruins of an ancient Cretaceous
Volcano from 130 million years ago! When the volcano ran out of magma, the heavy mountain
peak imploded into itself, creating a massive caldera bowl. Magma squeezed up through the
circular fractures around the edge, cooling underground into an exceptionally dense, iron-hard
rock called monzonite. Over millions of years, the softer surrounding landscape eroded away,
leaving the circular ring of magma-hardened mountains behind.
🧊 Ice Age Boulders: During the last Ice Age, a mile-thick sheet of moving ice plucked
massive, house-sized blocks of granite directly out of the mountain walls, carrying them south.
When the glaciers melted, they dropped these colossal rocks haphazardly through the forest.
These are known as Glacial Erratics, and Pawtuckaway contains one of the premier boulder
fields in North America, forming deep, cold stone labyrinths in the middle of the woods.
📜 Ancient Culture & Stonework: Long before colonial logging, these deep rock crevices were
sacred sanctuaries for the Pennacook Abenaki Native Americans, who utilized the stone
fractures as defensive blinds and shelters. In the 1700s, colonial timber operators moved in to
feed lumber sawmills along the river networks. If you hike deep into Big Island, you can locate
ancient, overgrown colonial stone walls and tiny, iron-fenced 18th-century family homestead
cemeteries buried completely beneath the pine needles.
